## Regulatory Framework

The TN Legal Services website must comply with the following legal and regulatory requirements:

### 1. **Law Society of Ontario (LSO) Rules**

- **Rule 4.2** - Marketing and Advertising
  - Must not make false, misleading, or unverifiable claims
  - Must not guarantee results
  - Must identify lawyers and their jurisdictions
  - Website communications must be professional and dignified

- **Rule 3.2** - Confidentiality
  - Clear disclaimer that website contact does not create solicitor-client privilege
  - No unsolicited information should be treated as confidential

- **Rule 3.4** - Client Files and Records
  - File retention requirements (minimum 10 years)
  - Privacy and security obligations

### 2. **Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)**

Canada's federal privacy law governing how private sector organizations collect, use, and disclose personal information:

- **Principle 1:** Accountability
- **Principle 2:** Identifying Purposes
- **Principle 3:** Consent
- **Principle 4:** Limiting Collection
- **Principle 5:** Limiting Use, Disclosure, and Retention
- **Principle 6:** Accuracy
- **Principle 7:** Safeguards
- **Principle 8:** Openness
- **Principle 9:** Individual Access
- **Principle 10:** Challenging Compliance

### 3. **Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA)**

- Website should be WCAG 2.0 Level AA compliant
- Accessibility statement recommended

### 4. **Copyright Act (Canada)**

- Proper attribution for third-party content
- Respect for Crown copyright on government materials
- Protection of firm's intellectual property
